Twitter Image Fix

The Issue

Twitter has a habit of destroying image quality everywhere you look, in tweets and on your profile.
This site tries its best to prepare your image in a way that preserves its quality when uploaded to twitter by using a few tricks.

Currently only profile pictures and banners is supported but eventually you'll be able to do tweet images too.

Avatar Profile Banner

How It Works

It's possible for avatars and banners to avoid being compressed by Twitter when uploading the exact resolution (400x400 for avatars, 1500x500 for banners) as an 8-bit PNG.

You can read more on the official guidance for uploading high resolution images to twitter on this official forum post.

What About Tweets?

As of writing Twitter currently leaves any JPEG you upload alone, so uploading your PNG as a high quality JPEG (I promise that exists) will get much better results. Thumbnails will still look compressed on some platforms but if a user clicks onto the full image they'll get it at full resolution.

Starting today, Twitter will preserve JPEGs as they are encoded for upload on Twitter for Web. (Caveat, cannot have EXIF orientation)
